Question 1

2016 P1 Q19
Addition polymer monomer: restore the C=C bond in the repeat unit; attached groups stay
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
The repeat unit has one backbone carbon bonded to CH₃ and H, and the other bonded to H and Cl. The monomer must have the same attached groups around a C=C bond — so the monomer is CH₂=CClCH₃ (or equivalent structure).

Question 2

2016 P1 Q20
Ethanol + acidified KMnO₄ → ethanoic acid (oxidation of primary alcohol)
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Ethanol is a primary alcohol and is oxidised to ethanoic acid by acidified potassium manganate(VII). Ethane and ethene are not oxidation products of ethanol, and methanol is a different alcohol.

Question 3

2017 P1 Q20
Ethanol: complete combustion → CO₂ + H₂O; oxidised by KMnO₄; no C=O bond in alcohol
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Statement 1 false: complete combustion of ethanol produces CO₂ (not CO). Statement 2 true: ethanol can be oxidised by acidified KMnO₄. Statement 3 false: ethanol does not contain a C=O bond (that is the carbonyl group in aldehydes/ketones). Statement 4 true. So statements 2 and 4 are correct.

Question 4

2018 P1 Q19
Addition polymers form from unsaturated monomers (C=C); monomers decolourise bromine water
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Since the polymer is formed by addition polymerisation, the monomer must be unsaturated (contains a C=C bond). An unsaturated monomer reacts with aqueous bromine, changing it from reddish-brown to colourless.

Question 5

2018 P1 Q20
Ethanol → propanol cannot be done in a single reaction (different carbon chain lengths)
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Ethanol → ethanoic acid: one oxidation step (✓). Ethene → poly(ethene): one polymerisation step (✓). Glucose → ethanol + CO₂: one fermentation step (✓). Ethanol → propanol: not possible in a single reaction in this syllabus — they have different carbon chain lengths.

Question 6

2019 P1 Q19
During addition polymerisation, the C=C opens; attached groups remain on the same carbons
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
The monomer has CH₃ and Cl on one carbon of the C=C, and two H atoms on the other. After polymerisation, the repeat unit must show one backbone carbon carrying CH₃ and Cl, and the other backbone carbon carrying two H atoms.

Question 7

2019 P1 Q20
Acidified KMnO₄ (purple) oxidises alcohols → colour change to colourless
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Ethanol is an alcohol and can be oxidised by acidified potassium manganate(VII), causing it to decolourise (purple → colourless). Ethane, ethanoic acid and poly(ethene) do not give this oxidation reaction.

Question 8

2020 P1 Q38
Monomer of addition polymer: same atoms as repeat unit + one C=C bond; alkenes = CₙH₂ₙ
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
The repeat unit has 4 carbon atoms. Addition polymerisation does not change the number of atoms — the monomer has the same atoms. With 4 carbons and one C=C bond (alkene), the formula is CₙH₂ₙ = C₄H₈.

Question 9

2021 P1 Q20
KMnO₄: oxidising agent, Mr = 158, oxidises ethanol → ethanoic acid
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Acidified KMnO₄ oxidises ethanol to ethanoic acid — this is correct. KMnO₄ has Mr = 39 + 55 + (16×4) = 158, not 126. It is an oxidising agent (not reducing). Sulfur dioxide decolourises acidified KMnO₄, not the reverse.

Question 10

2022 P1 Q19
To find monomer: place C=C between the two backbone carbons of the repeat unit
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Each backbone carbon in the repeat unit has one H and one CH₃ attached. Placing a C=C between them gives CH₃CH=CHCH₃ (but-2-ene). This monomer has a CH₃ and H attached to each carbon of the double bond.

Question 11

2022 P1 Q20
Ethanol oxidation by KMnO₄: KMnO₄ is reduced (purple → colourless)
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
During oxidation of ethanol, KMnO₄ acts as the oxidising agent and is itself reduced. The observed colour change is purple → colourless. Ethanol is oxidised to ethanoic acid.

Question 12

2023 P1 Q19
Alcohols: oxidised slowly by atmospheric O₂ to carboxylic acids; do not decolourise bromine water
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Alcohols do not decolourise bromine water (that tests for C=C bonds). Acidified KMnO₄ is decolourised (turns colourless) — it does not turn purple. Alcohols do not undergo addition with hydrogen. The correct statement: alcohols can be oxidised slowly by atmospheric oxygen to form carboxylic acids.

Question 13

2024 P1 Q19
Monomer from repeat unit with OH: restore C=C; OH group stays attached to same carbon
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
The repeat unit has an OH group on every alternate carbon in the backbone. Restoring the C=C bond between the two backbone carbons gives CH₂=CHOH as the monomer. The OH group remains attached to one carbon of the double bond after polymerisation.
⚡ One-line Takeaway

Identify the functional group first: alcohols are oxidised to carboxylic acids by acidified KMnO₄ (purple → colourless); addition polymer monomers are found by putting the C=C bond back into the repeat unit.
