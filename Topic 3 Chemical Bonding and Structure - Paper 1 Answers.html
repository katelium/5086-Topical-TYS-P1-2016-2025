Question 3

2017 P1 Q4
Ionic solids do NOT conduct electricity — ions are fixed in the lattice
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Sodium chloride exists as a giant ionic lattice held together by strong electrostatic forces between Na⁺ and Cl⁻ ions, giving it a high melting point. In the solid state the ions are fixed in position and cannot move, so solid NaCl does not conduct electricity. It only conducts when molten or dissolved in water.

Question 5

2018 P1 Q4
Covalent bond = shared pair of electrons between non-metal atoms
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
A covalent bond forms when two non-metal atoms each contribute one electron to a shared pair. Electrons are not transferred (that is ionic bonding). Metallic bonding involves a sea of delocalised electrons, not shared pairs between specific atoms.

Question 6

2019 P1 Q4
Ionic compounds: high melting point, giant lattice, mobile ions when molten/dissolved
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Sodium chloride contains Na⁺ and Cl⁻ ions arranged in a giant ionic lattice, dissolves in water, and has a high melting point (801 °C) due to strong electrostatic forces between oppositely charged ions. A low melting point is a property of simple molecular substances, not ionic compounds — this is the incorrect statement.

Question 7

2020 P1 Q5
Metal + non-metal → ionic compound with high melting point
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
A metal (A) reacting with a non-metal (B) forms an ionic compound. The compound contains ionic bonds — not covalent bonds. Ionic compounds have high melting points because a large amount of energy is needed to overcome the strong electrostatic forces of attraction between the oppositely charged ions.

💡 Explanation
In the chlorine-oxygen compound Cl₂O, oxygen has 6 outer electrons and forms 2 covalent bonds; each chlorine has 7 outer electrons and forms 1 covalent bond. The correct dot-and-cross diagram shows oxygen in the centre bonded to two chlorine atoms, each bond being a shared pair of electrons, with lone pairs on each atom.

Question 9

2021 P1 Q15
Non-metals: poor conductors, form acidic oxides, often have low melting points
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Good electrical and thermal conductivity are metallic properties. A typical non-metal is a poor conductor and its oxide dissolves in water to form an acidic solution (acidic oxide). Many non-metals also have relatively low melting points compared to metals. The pair "low melting point + forms an acidic oxide" best describes a non-metallic element.

Question 11

2022 P1 Q5
Ionic substances conduct when molten or in solution — ions become mobile
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Aqueous magnesium chloride conducts electricity because the Mg²⁺ and Cl⁻ ions are free to move in solution. Metal atoms lose electrons (not gain) to form positive ions. Non-metals typically form negative ions by gaining electrons. Sodium ions (Na⁺, 10 electrons) and argon atoms (Ar, 18 electrons) do not have the same electronic configuration.

Question 13

2023 P1 Q5
Ga₂S₃: Ga³⁺ and S²⁻ ions; ionic → high melting point
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
In Ga₂S₃, the charges must balance: 2 × (+3) from Ga³⁺ ions = +6, and 3 × (−2) from S²⁻ ions = −6. The formula confirms Ga forms a 3+ ion and S forms a 2− ion. Since gallium sulfide is ionic, it has a high melting point due to strong electrostatic forces between the oppositely charged ions.

Question 14

2024 P1 Q5
Mg loses 2e⁻ → Mg²⁺; each Cl gains 1e⁻ → Cl⁻; two Cl atoms needed for MgCl₂
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
During the formation of magnesium chloride, one magnesium atom loses two electrons to form Mg²⁺. Two chlorine atoms each gain one of those electrons, each forming Cl⁻. The two electrons transferred from magnesium are shared between the two chloride ions, giving the formula MgCl₂.

Question 15

2024 P1 Q6
NH₃: N forms 3 bonds with 3 H atoms + 1 lone pair on N
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Ammonia (NH₃) has nitrogen in the centre with 5 outer electrons. Nitrogen forms three covalent bonds — one with each hydrogen atom — using 3 of those electrons in shared pairs. The remaining 2 electrons form one lone pair on the nitrogen atom. The correct dot-and-cross diagram shows three N–H shared pairs and one lone pair on N.
⚡ One-line Takeaway

Metal + non-metal → ionic (high mp, conducts when molten/dissolved); non-metal + non-metal → covalent (shared pairs); alloys = metallic lattice with mixed atom sizes; use valence electrons to predict formula and dot-and-cross diagrams.
