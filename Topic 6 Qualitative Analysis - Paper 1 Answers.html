Fe³⁺ → red-brown ppt with NaOH; NO₃⁻ → NH₃ gas with warm NaOH + Al
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
The red-brown precipitate with aqueous sodium hydroxide identifies Fe³⁺ ions. Together, Fe³⁺ and NO₃⁻ identify solution Q as iron(III) nitrate.

Question 2

2018 P1 Q2
Fe³⁺ gives a red-brown precipitate with NaOH — NOT a green precipitate
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Solution X turns red litmus paper blue, meaning it is alkaline. Iron(III) ions (Fe³⁺) produce a red-brown precipitate with aqueous sodium hydroxide — not green. A green precipitate with sodium hydroxide would indicate Fe²⁺ ions. The statement claiming a green precipitate with iron(III) nitrate is therefore incorrect.

Question 3

2020 P1 Q3
SO₄²⁻ → white ppt with acidified Ba(NO₃)₂; Ca²⁺ → white ppt with NaOH, insoluble in excess
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
The white precipitate with acidified barium nitrate is barium sulfate (BaSO₄), confirming sulfate ions (SO₄²⁻). The white precipitate with sodium hydroxide that does not dissolve in excess indicates calcium ions (Ca²⁺) — unlike zinc hydroxide or aluminium hydroxide, calcium hydroxide does not dissolve in excess NaOH. Together these identify W as calcium sulfate.

Question 4

2021 P1 Q2
Cl⁻ → white ppt with acidified AgNO₃; Ca²⁺ → white ppt with NaOH, insoluble in excess
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
The white precipitate with acidified silver nitrate is silver chloride (AgCl), confirming chloride ions (Cl⁻). The white precipitate with sodium hydroxide that is insoluble in excess points to calcium ions — zinc hydroxide would dissolve in excess NaOH, but calcium hydroxide does not. The compound is therefore calcium chloride (CaCl₂).

Question 5

2023 P1 Q2
NH₄Cl: no reaction with HCl; NH₃ gas with warm NaOH; white ppt with acidified AgNO₃
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Ammonium chloride gives no visible change with dilute hydrochloric acid. When warmed with aqueous sodium hydroxide, ammonium ions produce ammonia gas, which turns damp red litmus paper blue. With acidified silver nitrate, chloride ions give a white precipitate of silver chloride. This combination of results uniquely identifies ammonium chloride solution.

Question 6

2024 P1 Q10
NH₄⁺ + warm NaOH → NH₃ gas; NH₃ turns damp red litmus blue
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
When solution S is heated with aqueous sodium hydroxide, a gas G is produced that changes the colour of damp red litmus paper — this means the gas is alkaline. The only gas that does this in qualitative analysis is ammonia (NH₃). Ammonia is produced when ammonium ions (NH₄⁺) react with sodium hydroxide on warming: NH₄⁺ + OH⁻ → NH₃ + H₂O.
⚡ One-line Takeaway

Fe³⁺ → red-brown ppt with NaOH; SO₄²⁻ → white ppt with acidified Ba(NO₃)₂; Cl⁻ → white ppt with acidified AgNO₃; NH₄⁺ → NH₃ gas (turns damp red litmus blue) with warm NaOH; Ca²⁺ → white ppt insoluble in excess NaOH.
