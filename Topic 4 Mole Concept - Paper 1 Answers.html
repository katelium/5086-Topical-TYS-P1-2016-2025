moles = mass ÷ Ar; gas volume at r.t.p. = moles × 24 dm³
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Equation: 2Ca + O₂ → 2CaO. Moles of Ca = 2.0 ÷ 40 = 0.050 mol. From the 2:1 ratio, moles of O₂ = 0.050 ÷ 2 = 0.025 mol. Volume of O₂ at r.t.p. = 0.025 × 24 = 0.60 dm³.

Question 2

2016 P1 Q6
Balance charges in the formula: SO₄²⁻ × 3 = −6, so Fe must be 3+
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
In Fe₂(SO₄)₃, there are 3 sulfate ions (SO₄²⁻), giving a total negative charge of −6. The two iron ions must provide +6 to balance, so each iron ion is Fe³⁺. The ions present are Fe³⁺ and SO₄²⁻.

Question 5

2018 P1 Q5
Gas volumes react in mole ratio; water is liquid at r.t.p. so not counted
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Equation: C₂H₄ + 3O₂ → 2CO₂ + 2H₂O. 20 cm³ ethene requires 3 × 20 = 60 cm³ O₂, leaving 70 − 60 = 10 cm³ O₂ unreacted. 20 cm³ ethene produces 2 × 20 = 40 cm³ CO₂. Water is liquid at r.t.p. so it contributes no gas volume. Total gas remaining = 40 + 10 = 50 cm³.

Dot-and-cross diagrams show only outer-shell electrons, not all electrons in the molecule
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Dot-and-cross diagrams only show outer-shell (valence) electrons. The molecule AB₃ contains many more electrons in total than just the outer shell electrons shown. A statement claiming the molecule contains only 8 electrons in total is incorrect — 8 is merely the number of outer-shell electrons around atom A after bonding.

Question 13

2022 P1 Q6
moles MgCl₂ → × 2 (ratio) → moles AgCl → × Mr(AgCl) = mass
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Equation: MgCl₂ + 2AgNO₃ → 2AgCl + Mg(NO₃)₂. Mr of MgCl₂ = 24 + 2(35.5) = 95. Moles of MgCl₂ = 3.80 ÷ 95 = 0.040 mol. From the 1:2 ratio, moles of AgCl = 0.080 mol. Mr of AgCl = 108 + 35.5 = 143.5. Mass of AgCl = 0.080 × 143.5 = 11.48 g ≈ 11.5 g.

💡 Explanation
Total moles of NaCl = 5.85 ÷ 58.5 = 0.100 mol in 100 cm³ = 0.100 dm³, giving a concentration of 1.0 mol/dm³. When the solution is poured into two beakers (90 cm³ and 10 cm³), the concentration remains 1.0 mol/dm³ in both — only the amount (moles) in each portion differs, not the concentration.

Question 16

2024 P1 Q7
mass S = compound − metal; moles S → × 2 (M₂S ratio) → moles M → Ar = mass ÷ moles
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Mass of sulfur = 32.0 − 25.6 = 6.4 g. Moles of S = 6.4 ÷ 32 = 0.20 mol. In M₂S, the M:S ratio is 2:1, so moles of M = 2 × 0.20 = 0.40 mol. Ar of M = 25.6 ÷ 0.40 = 64.
⚡ One-line Takeaway

Always convert cm³ ÷ 1000 to dm³, use moles = c × V or mass ÷ Mr, apply the balanced equation ratio, and remember that dividing a solution doesn't change its concentration.
