Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Astatine is at the bottom of Group VII → expected to be a black solid. Since astatine is less reactive than bromine, it cannot displace bromine from potassium bromide solution.

Question 3

2017 P1 Q11
Elements are arranged in order of increasing proton number
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Elements are arranged by increasing proton number (not nucleon number). Elements in the same period do not have the same outer shell electrons — they have the same number of shells. Group I reactivity increases (not decreases) down the group.

Question 4

2017 P1 Q12
Group VII reactivity decreases down the group; more reactive halogen displaces less reactive
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Iodine cannot displace bromine (iodine is less reactive than bromine). Chlorine can displace iodine from iodide solution (chlorine is more reactive than iodine). Bromine is a liquid at room temperature, not a solid.

Question 5

Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Down Group I: number of outer shell electrons stays the same (always 1); number of electron shells increases; reactivity with water increases. The property that decreases down the group is melting point.

Question 7

Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
T has a lower melting point (63 °C) than R (98 °C) → T is lower down Group I. Therefore R has fewer electron shells than T. R is not more reactive than T — T is lower and more reactive.

Question 8

Across a period left → right: metals to non-metals; proton number increases
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
X is a metal → on the left of the period. Y is a non-metal → further to the right. Since proton number increases left to right across a period, Y has a higher atomic number than X.

Question 9

2021 P1 Q14
Halogens react with sodium; noble gases are unreactive; halogens darken down the group
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Q (pale yellow gas, forms NaQ) is a halogen above R. R (dark solid, forms NaR) is a halogen below Q — halogens darken going down Group VII. S (colourless gas, no product with sodium) is a noble gas — it is unreactive.

Question 11

R has 12 protons → magnesium (2,8,2). Q has 20 protons → calcium (2,8,8,2). Q has more electron shells than R. Both have 2 outer shell electrons → same group (Group II), not different groups.

Question 12

2023 P1 Q14
Bromine is a red-brown liquid; more reactive than iodine → displaces iodide ions
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Bromine is a red-brown liquid at room temperature (not a gas). Iodine is less reactive than bromine, so iodine cannot displace bromine from bromide solution. Bromine can react with potassium iodide solution, displacing the less reactive iodide.

Question 14

2024 P1 Q13
Group I: low density, alkaline with water, melting point decreases and reactivity increases down the group
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Statement 1: true — Group I metals form alkaline solutions with water. Statement 2: false — Group I metals generally have low densities (lithium, sodium, and potassium all float on water). Statement 3: false — potassium is more reactive than lithium, not less. Statement 4: true — sodium has a lower melting point than lithium. Statements 1 and 4 are correct.
⚡ One-line Takeaway

Same group = same outer shell electrons; same period = same number of shells. Group I gets more reactive going down; Group VII gets less reactive going down.
