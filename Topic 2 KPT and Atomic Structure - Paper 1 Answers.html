Question 1

2017 P1 Q1
A substance is liquid when temp > melting point AND temp < boiling point
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Bromine is a liquid at 20 °C. This means its melting point must be below 20 °C (so it has already melted) and its boiling point must be above 20 °C (so it has not yet boiled). Only a melting point of −7 °C and a boiling point of 59 °C satisfy both conditions simultaneously.

Question 2

2021 P1 Q3
Particles lose energy during freezing (liquid→solid) and condensation (gas→liquid)
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Particles lose energy during freezing and condensation. The correct diagram must show particles becoming more closely packed and more orderly — for example, liquid changing to solid. In this change of state, particles release energy to the surroundings. Melting, boiling, and sublimation all involve particles gaining energy, not losing it.

Question 3

2024 P1 Q3
During melting: particles absorb energy, overcome forces of attraction, move further apart
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
When a solid melts, particles absorb energy and use it to overcome some of the forces of attraction between them. They change from vibrating about fixed positions (solid) to moving and sliding past one another (liquid), and become slightly further apart. The particles gain kinetic energy and the arrangement becomes less ordered.
⚡ One-line Takeaway

A substance is liquid when temp is above its melting point but below its boiling point; solid→liquid or liquid→gas means particles gain energy, while gas→liquid or liquid→solid means particles lose energy.
