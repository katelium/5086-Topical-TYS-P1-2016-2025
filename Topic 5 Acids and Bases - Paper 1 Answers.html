Question 1

2016 P1 Q10
Amphoteric oxides react with BOTH acids AND alkalis
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Zinc oxide is amphoteric. It behaves as a base when reacting with hydrochloric acid (ZnO + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂O) and as an acid when reacting with sodium hydroxide (ZnO + 2NaOH → Na₂ZnO₂ + H₂O). The correct statement is that it reacts with both an acid and an alkali.

Question 2

2017 P1 Q9
To change acidic soil to alkaline → add an alkali (e.g. Ca(OH)₂)
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Hydrangea flowers are blue in acidic soil but pink in alkaline soil. To change the soil from acidic to alkaline, an alkali must be added. Calcium hydroxide (Ca(OH)₂, also known as slaked lime) is an alkali that neutralises the acidic soil and raises its pH. Citric acid would make the soil more acidic; copper(II) sulfate and sodium chloride are not alkalis.

Question 3

2018 P1 Q10
Acidic oxides come from NON-metals; metal oxides are usually basic
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Acidic oxides are typically formed by non-metals (e.g. CO₂, SO₂, NO₂). Metal oxides are usually basic, not acidic. The statement claiming that acidic oxides are formed by metals is incorrect. Acids do react with metal oxides to form salt and water, and alkalis do produce OH⁻ ions in solution — those are correct statements.

Question 4

2018 P1 Q11
Ca(OH)₂ neutralises acidic soil → raises pH
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Calcium hydroxide is an alkali. When added to acidic soil, it neutralises the excess acid, raising the pH of the soil to a level more suitable for plant growth. This is the agricultural practice of "liming" the soil.

Question 5

Reacts with acid but NOT alkali → basic oxide → X is a metal
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
The oxide of X reacts with hydrochloric acid (like a base) but does not react with sodium hydroxide. This is the behaviour of a basic oxide. Basic oxides are typically metal oxides. Therefore, X is a metal and its oxide is a basic oxide.

Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
When an acid reacts with a reactive metal, the products are a salt and hydrogen gas. When an acid reacts with a carbonate, the products are a salt, water, and carbon dioxide. The correct row must show "salt + hydrogen" for the metal reaction and "salt + water + carbon dioxide" for the carbonate reaction.

Question 8

💡 Explanation
Liquid soap has pH 8, which is alkaline. Turmeric turns red in alkaline conditions. Vinegar has pH 3, which is acidic. Turmeric remains yellow in acidic conditions. So liquid soap turns turmeric red, and vinegar keeps it yellow.

Question 11

2024 P1 Q9
Basic oxides react with acids → salt + water; do NOT react with other bases
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Basic oxides neutralise acids to form a salt and water (e.g. CuO + H₂SO₄ → CuSO₄ + H₂O). Since basic oxides are themselves basic in nature, they do not react with other bases or alkalis. The correct row shows that a basic oxide reacts with an acid but does not react with a base.
⚡ One-line Takeaway

Acids react with metals (→ H₂), carbonates (→ CO₂), and bases/alkalis (→ salt + water); alkalis are soluble bases; amphoteric oxides react with both acids and alkalis; acidic oxides come from non-metals, basic oxides from metals.
