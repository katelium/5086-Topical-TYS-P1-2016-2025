Question 2

2016 P1 Q17
Incomplete combustion of fuels → carbon monoxide (toxic)
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
CO₂ and H₂O are products of complete combustion. Oxides of nitrogen form at high temperature in engines from N₂ and O₂ — not from incomplete combustion. Carbon monoxide is the toxic gas produced when petrol burns with insufficient oxygen (incomplete combustion).

Question 3

2017 P1 Q15
Dry air: ~78% N₂, ~21% O₂, ~1% noble gases + trace CO₂
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Nitrogen is the largest component of dry air (~78%). Oxygen is about one-fifth (~21%). The remaining small percentage (~1%) is mainly noble gases with a trace amount of carbon dioxide.

Question 4

2017 P1 Q16
High temperatures (lightning & engines) cause N₂ + O₂ → oxides of nitrogen
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Both lightning and internal combustion engines produce extremely high temperatures. This causes nitrogen and oxygen in the air to react, forming oxides of nitrogen (including NO and NO₂). Both sources produce the same pollutant by the same mechanism.

Question 6

2018 P1 Q16
NO₂ and SO₂ form acid rain → erodes buildings; CO and CH₄ do not
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Carbon monoxide is toxic but does not cause acid rain or erosion of buildings. Methane is a greenhouse gas. Nitrogen dioxide and sulfur dioxide dissolve in rainwater to form acidic rain, which chemically erodes limestone and marble buildings.

Question 8

2020 P1 Q17
Heated copper removes O₂ from air; ~21% O₂ is absorbed, leaving ~79% behind
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Heated copper reacts with oxygen (2Cu + O₂ → 2CuO), removing it from the air. 21% of 50 cm³ = ~10.5 cm³ of O₂ removed. Final volume ≈ 50 − 10.5 = 39.5 cm³, close to 40 cm³. The graph should show volume decreasing from 50 cm³ and levelling off at ~40 cm³.

Question 10

Select the answer from your copy of the paper:

Question 12

2024 P1 Q20
Incomplete combustion of fossil fuels → carbon monoxide (not CO₂)
Select the answer from your copy of the paper:
💡 Explanation
Carbon dioxide is produced in complete combustion. Nitrogen dioxide forms when N₂ and O₂ react at high temperatures (not from incomplete combustion). Sulfur dioxide forms from sulfur impurities in fuels — not from incomplete combustion of carbon. Carbon monoxide is the product of incomplete combustion when there is insufficient oxygen.
⚡ One-line Takeaway

Dry air is ~78% N₂, 21% O₂, 1% other gases; incomplete combustion → CO (toxic); NO₂ and SO₂ → acid rain (erodes buildings); argon is the most abundant noble gas.
